## ai_ml | Supervised Learning and Generalization

Tom Mitchell frames machine learning around improving performance on a task through experience. In supervised learning, the learner receives labeled examples and searches for a hypothesis that generalizes beyond the training set. Strong interviews should explore the candidate's understanding of inductive bias, overfitting, validation, and why a model that memorizes training examples can fail on new data.

## ai_ml | Model Evaluation and Error Analysis

Applied machine learning requires separating training, validation, and test feedback. A candidate should reason about metrics, data leakage, class imbalance, feature quality, and the difference between statistical improvement and product impact. Error analysis should connect failed predictions to concrete remediation steps such as collecting better labels, adding features, changing thresholds, or simplifying the model.

## data_science | Practical ML Workflow

Introduction to Machine Learning with Python emphasizes iterative data understanding, preprocessing, model selection, evaluation, and communication. Applied candidates should explain how they inspect data, handle categorical variables and missing values, compare baselines, and present limitations clearly to stakeholders.

## data_science | Algorithm Selection

Brownlee-style applied ML focuses on choosing algorithms by problem type, assumptions, data volume, interpretability needs, and evaluation constraints. A strong answer compares simple baselines with more complex models and explains how cross-validation, learning curves, and feature importance can guide decisions.

## advanced_ml | Probabilistic Pattern Recognition

Pattern recognition often models uncertainty explicitly. Candidates should be able to discuss likelihood, priors, posterior inference, decision boundaries, and the relationship between probabilistic assumptions and classification behavior. Strong answers connect theory to implementation tradeoffs.

## advanced_ml | Deep Learning Optimization

Deep learning systems depend on representation learning, loss functions, gradient-based optimization, regularization, and careful validation. Interview questions should probe whether a candidate can diagnose underfitting, overfitting, unstable training, and deployment mismatch.

## backend | Service Architecture for AI Systems

Backend services supporting AI workflows should separate API concerns from business logic, retrieval, model orchestration, and persistence. A well-designed service validates inputs, records traceable state transitions, handles failures, and exposes clear lifecycle endpoints for creating sessions, submitting answers, and retrieving summaries.

## backend | Persistence and Traceability

AI interview systems should persist uploaded resume metadata, extracted profile signals, retrieved context, generated questions, answers, feedback, and final summaries. Traceability matters because reviewers need to understand why a question was asked and which knowledge source influenced it.
